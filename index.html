Men’s basketball defeats Delaware State, Lehigh University to continue winning streak

By Takashi Williams and Benjamin Rubin. Graphic by Wilson Zhen.

November 15, 2024

Men’s basketball is enjoying a dreamlike start to its 2024-25 campaign. Longtime fans of the Light Blue might be experiencing déjàvu from the 2005-06 season, which was the last time the Lions started a season 4-0.

“Every game has been interesting in some way,” head coach Jim Engles said. “We’ve been able to win them, and we’re still learning. So it’s a great credit to the guys. … I really do believe we’re building that consistency.”

The Light Blue (4-0) had its first game on the floor of Levien Gymnasium this past Saturday against Delaware State University (1-2), dominating the Hornets in an 83-62 win.

The game proved to be a well-rounded scoring affair for the Lions, with four of the Light Blue’s starters—senior guard Geronimo Rubio De La Rosa, junior guard Avery Brown, and junior forwards Zine Eddine Bedri and Blair Thompson—all scoring in the double digits. Thompson also registered the first double-double for the Lions this season and of his collegiate career.

While Engles acknowledged after the Villanova upset win that there would be a target on his team’s back moving forward this season, the Lions came out hungrier than ever. Brown produced an early rejection to fire up his squad.

On the next possession, Bedri used nifty footwork to back his man down in the paint to score the Lions’ first points of the night. He later pump-faked at the perimeter before driving into the paint with featherweight steps to give the Lions a 9-6 lead early on.

The Lions showed impressive ball movement, leading to three pointers from both senior guard Jaden Cooper and first-year guard Gianni Cobb to leave the Hornets behind in the points tally.

Thompson combined for 23 points, 18 rebounds, and three steals throughout the first two home games of the season. Photo by Giada Robinson / Photo Trainee

Thompson showed skill from the perimeter, with a big triple forcing a time-out from the Hornets and pulling the Lions to a 33-20 lead. The New York native finished the first half shooting 3-4 from the perimeter with 9 points and six rebounds, as the Light Blue ended the half up 42-28.

The Lions continued to pull away after the break, with the first points of the second half coming from Rubio De La Rosa on a triple, followed by an easy lay-up set up by a crafty in-bounds play, making their lead 58-37.

Rubio De La Rosa, the Lou Henson National Player of the Week, finished the game with 19 points, six rebounds, and two steals. The Light Blue went on to maintain its lead and secure its third straight win of the season, 83-62.

Following the win, Columbia stayed in New York for a Tuesday night matchup against Lehigh University (0-3). The Lions did not trail once, eventually closing out a nail-biting 76-75 victory behind clutch free throws from Brown.

In both games, the Lions had at least four players produce double-digit scoring performances. Photo by Giada Robinson / Photo Trainee

Rubio De La Rosa filled up the stat sheet once again, finishing with 24 points, eight rebounds, three assists, and three steals.

Bedri opened up the scoring against the Mountain Hawks with a triple to beat the shot clock. A couple possessions later, the Algeria native blew by his defender for another score, this time a thundering slam.

Lehigh responded well, matching Columbia’s early offensive prowess. The two teams traded baskets until halftime, as the Lions led 40-37 going into the break.

Rubio De La Rosa came back out of the locker room firing. He scored Columbia’s first 9 points of the half, including a patented backdoor finish off a dish from junior guard Kenny Noland, who finished with a game-high six assists, matching his career high.

With just over three minutes left in play, Columbia had a 13-point lead, their widest of the game. Then came Lehigh’s full-court pressure.

The Mountain Hawks tallied two steals in the press and scored 9 unanswered points over the next two minutes. In crunch time, they fouled, and the Lions struggled to cash in on free throws. Brown, who got fouled with 11 seconds left, went 1-2 from the line, giving Columbia a 75-72 lead.

After a quick inbound, Lehigh found the hot hand in Keith Higgins Jr., who put up 28 points throughout the contest. The guard hit his sixth triple of the half—his seventh total—to tie the game at 75 apiece.

With four seconds left in regulation, Brown received the Columbia inbound and dribbled coast-to-coast before being shoved to the floor by a Lehigh defender. Less than a second remained as Brown stepped up to the charity stripe for two more shots. He hit the first, putting the Lions up one, then intentionally missed the second, forcing a live-ball rebound that killed the clock. The home crowd breathed a sigh of relief as Columbia held on, 76-75.

Postgame, Rubio De La Rosa emphasized the team’s success over his own. “It’s not you,” Rubio De La Rosa said. “It’s we. We are four and o.”

Cooper was noticeably absent from Tuesday’s contest. Engles informed Spectator postgame that Cooper will be away from the team indefinitely for personal reasons.

“We obviously support him in every way possible, and we’re hoping to get him back at some point,” Engles said.

The Lions will be back in action at home against Mercyhurst University on Saturday. Tipoff is scheduled for noon, and the game will be streamed on ESPN+.
